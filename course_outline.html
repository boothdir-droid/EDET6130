Comprehensive Master Course Syllabus & Operational Curriculum Outline

Curriculum Architecture

This syllabus forms the programmatic operational roadmap for initializing student assistants within the AV Operations Department. Moving sequentially from basic human resources onboarding boundaries up through engineering concepts, physical hardware wiring mechanics, and complex system troubleshooting topologies, the outline establishes a verifiable competency standard for maintaining campus technological learning spaces.

Goal: Understand your role, responsibilities, and required training.

Curriculum Topics

Overview of the Department of AV Operations
  • Structure of Department & Key Personnel
  • Relationship to Other Departments
  • Core Departmental Responsibilities
  • Technology Enhanced Classroom (TEC) Support
  • AV Event Support, Setup and Breakdown
  • AV Equipment Maintenance, Design, & Installation
Expectations & Required Portals
  • Typical Student Responsibilities & "Day In The Life"
  • Common Tasks & Cross-Departmental Assistance
  • Required Training Accounts: Extron Electronics, AVIXA
  • LinkedIn Learning, Legrand AV, AtlasIED Academy, Coursera
  • Professional Expectations: Communication, Reliability, & Accountability

Module 3

AV Fundamentals & IT Integration

Goal: Build foundational understanding of AV terminology and crossover with IT Helpdesk and Networking Operations.

Curriculum Topics

Core Engineering Concepts
  • What Is an AV System? Component overview and macro function
  • Signal Fundamentals: Analog versus digital properties
  • Resolution, scanning formats, and signal levels
  • Inputs versus Outputs operational directional logic
  • Signal Processing parameters & Compatibility issues
AV + IT Convergence Mesh
  • Network-based AV streaming versus physical hardware extension
  • USB integration pathways, variations, and limitations
  • Network Control layers and localized computer issues
  • Key Technologies: EDID (Display communication handshakes)
  • HDCP parameters (Content protection protocol management)
Module 4

Cables & Connector Lab

Goal: Identify connectors and cable types and understand their role in a system.

Curriculum Topics

Connector Anatomy & Signaling
  • Connector Formats: HDMI, VGA, XLR, RCA, Speakon, USB
  • Interface attributes: Connector Gender and functionality
  • Audio Engineering: Balanced versus Unbalanced Audio lines
  • Digital vs. Analog signal transport frameworks
  • Signal Types: Audio vs. Video vs. Control vs. Ethernet
Laboratory Analysis
  • Common Failure Points: Loose connections and broken cables
  • Incorrectly connected lines / routing errors
  • Signal Degradation factors driven by structural cable length
  • Connector Identification Lab: Visual Recognition and Use-Case matching
  • Physical diagnostic troubleshooting exercises
Module 5

Typical AV System (Anatomy of a TEC)

Goal: Understand how real classroom systems are constructed.

Curriculum Topics

System Architecture & Hardware
  • Signal Path Journey: Source → Transport → Processing → Output + Control
  • Typical Source Devices: Laptops, PCs, Document Cameras (HDMI, USB-C)
  • Switching & Transport hardware: Extron IN1606 / IN1808 systems
  • DTP Transmission engineering and typical signal routing matrix
  • Audio Components: Mixers, Digital Signal Processors (DSPs), and Amplifiers
  • Acoustic Output: Speaker system typologies and wiring layouts
Output, Control, & Design
  • Output Hardware: Displays vs. Projectors, Screen relay systems, Local monitors
  • Real Classroom System Walkthrough: User guides and video references
  • Control layer mechanics: Relay screens, Network Button Panels (NBPs), Touchscreens
  • The structural role of the localized Control Processor engine
  • System Design Principles: Standardization, Reliability, and Maintainability
Module 6 (Primary Skill Module)

Troubleshooting

Goal: Develop structured troubleshooting ability.

Curriculum Topics

Methodology & Triage Sequences
  • Troubleshooting Script Lifecycle: Identify → Isolate → Test → Resolve
  • Step-by-Step Checking: Cables, Power continuity, Inputs/Outputs, Device state
  • Common Issue Patterns: No video, No audio, Intermittent signals, Sync errors
  • Tracing physical signal pathways sequentially across devices
  • Real Classroom Scenarios: Projector not displaying, Audio present with no video
Diagnostic Mindset Rules
  • Bypassing hard architecture blockages: Screen not lowering
  • Advanced Troubleshooting: HDCP green images, EDID mismatch, USB-C variables
  • Diagnostic Mindset Guidelines: Banish guessing from physical diagnostic fields
  • Follow the signal path and verify assumptions with systematic physical tests
  • Tracking documentation logs while testing to map remediation attempts
Module 7

Installation Best Practices

Goal: Understand the AV infrastructure needed and how a room is built. Follow best practices for safety and efficiency.

Curriculum Topics

Physical Fabrication
  • Installation Principles: Engineering planning, layout rules, clean system design
  • Rack Building standards: Component organization, internal thermal airflow, and cooling
  • Mounting & Hardware mechanics: Display brackets, projectors, and safety weight boundaries
Staging Standards
  • Cable Management layout rules: Labeling standards and routing techniques
  • Audio System Installation: Speaker placement and physical wiring practices
  • Common Installation Faults: Polarity errors, connector pinouts, cross-connected lines, network config

Advanced Skill Tracks

  • Advanced networking architecture and automated control system programming
  • Continuous Improvement framework: Institutional learning loops derived from system failures
