Course Learning Objectives — Behavioral & Technical Competency Framework

Instructional Outcomes Framework

This document defines the core behavioral learning objectives that student assistants must verify to achieve micro-credentials within the training program. Each objective is explicitly structured around measurable performance metrics, detailing the exact technical criteria required to satisfy field service, deployment maintenance, and critical troubleshooting tasks across our educational environments.

Module 1

Introduction to the AV Student Assistant Position

  • Identify Department Hierarchy: Map the internal structure and key personnel of the AV Operations Department and locate cross-departmental integration dependencies.
  • Configure External Training Portals: Authenticate, navigate, and log progress across mandatory educational training accounts including Extron, AVIXA, and Legrand AV.
  • Differentiate Core Service Domains: Explain operational boundaries dividing Technology Enhanced Classroom (TEC) support from live AV event setup lines.
Module 2

Administrative Procedures and Expectations

  • Execute Shift Protocols: Demonstrate rigorous start-of-shift preparation checklist rules and end-of-shift standard operational closeouts.
  • Apply Escalation Deadlines: Implement the strict 15-minute escalation rule via Microsoft Teams, correctly compiling all required critical room and symptom metadata.
  • Manage Timecard Compliance: Navigate the institutional timecard system to accurately record, track, and submit student service credit log shifts.
Module 3

AV Fundamentals & IT Integration

  • Analyze Signal Properties: Articulate foundational behavioral differences between analog and digital signals, processing resolution factors, and scanning types.
  • Diagnose Handshake Boundaries: Interrogate display interface variables to isolate performance conflicts caused by EDID mismatch or active HDCP content restrictions.
  • Differentiate Transport Vectors: Define core system constraints distinguishing network-based AV streaming protocols from raw physical copper line extension.
Module 4

Cables & Connector Lab

  • Identify Physical Formats: Visually recognize, label, and differentiate connector types including HDMI, USB-C, XLR, VGA, Speakon, and RCA.
  • Isolate Audio Signatures: Differentiate physical pinouts, electrical behavior, and noise immunity properties distinguishing balanced audio lines from unbalanced audio lines.
  • Detect Line Degradation: Calculate expected signal transmission drop-off limits based on mechanical cable wear, loose interface shells, and strict run-length restrictions.
Module 5

Typical AV System (Anatomy of a TEC)

  • Trace Architecture Flows: Trace the complete active signal path within a standardized lecture hall, moving from Source through Transport, Processing, and Output stages.
  • Configure Control Engines: Explain the structural relationship between localized control processors, Network Button Panels (NBPs), relay screens, and graphical touch interfaces.
  • Map Audio Hardware Matrices: Match classroom input devices (microphones, PCs) straight into corresponding mixer, DSP, and amplifier output routing lines.
Module 6 (Primary Skill Module)

Troubleshooting

  • Apply Systematic Diagnosis: Execute the formalized diagnostic triage cycle (Identify → Isolate → Test → Resolve) instead of unsystematic guessing.
  • Isolate Common Fault Patterns: Analyze colloquial system fault descriptions to rapidly locate and remedy hard lockouts, total video drop-outs, sync errors, and green screens.
  • Log Experimental Progress: Document live hardware adjustments methodically as you test to explain exactly what path iterations were attempted.
Module 7

Installation Best Practices

  • Engineer Equipment Racks: Apply clean system design constraints to arrange hardware layout organization, thermal cooling parameters, and cable routing.
  • Implement Labeling Standards: Deploy uniform departmental cable marking conventions and wire bundling patterns to secure lines cleanly.
  • Verify Structural Mounting: Execute safe display, projector, and loudspeaker mounting layouts following strict structural weight and building safety boundaries.
Module 8

Ongoing Professional Development

  • Map Professional Pathways: Correlate internal department skills with broader industry roles including AV Field Technician, Help Desk Associate, or Project Manager.
  • Trace Certification Metrics: Formulate personal preparation strategies to clear advanced industry validation tiers such as Extron AV Associate or Audinate Dante Network standards.
  • Formulate Quality Feedback Loops: Analyze system failure reports and field errors to continually optimize local maintenance checklists.
