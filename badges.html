Digital Badge Credential Architecture & Micro-Learning Pathways

Micro-Credentialing Design Framework

To incentivize skill mastery and standardize technical operational quality within the AV Operations Department, this micro-credentialing architecture maps digital badges directly to discrete training modules. Student assistants earn these badges sequentially upon verifying physical proficiency, software configuration agility, and programmatic client service communication standards.

Module 1 Credential module_1_badge.png

Introduction to the AV Student Assistant Position

Understand the structural role, core responsibilities, and required training platforms (Extron, AVIXA, LinkedIn Learning, Legrand AV). Covers basic department layout, key personnel, technology enhanced classroom support, and professional communication expectations.

Department Structure Training Portals Professional Expectations
Module 2 Credential module_2_badge.png

Administrative Procedures and Expectations

Mastery of operational logistics, shift procedures, scheduling/HR requirements, and the timecard system. Focuses on communication rules, Microsoft Teams standards, and the 15-minute Escalation Protocol.

Shift Procedures Escalation Protocol Microsoft Teams
Module 3 Credential module_3_badge.png

AV Fundamentals & IT Integration

Builds foundational understanding of AV terminology, signal fundamentals (analog vs digital, resolution, inputs vs outputs), and convergence with IT networking operations. Explores critical technical protocols such as EDID display communication and HDCP content protection.

Signal Logic EDID & HDCP AV/IT Convergence
Module 4 Credential module_4_badge.png

Cables & Connector Lab

Hands-on identification and recognition of physical connectors (HDMI, VGA, XLR, RCA, Speakon, USB) and signaling types. Teaches common structural failure points like cable degradation, length limits, and loose interface joints.

Connector Matching Signal Types Physical Diagnostics
Module 5 Credential module_5_badge.png

Typical AV System (Anatomy of a TEC)

Deep dive into the architecture of real classroom systems following the explicit signal journey: Source → Transport → Processing → Output + Control. Covers hardware components like laptops, podium PCs, and document cameras.

System Architecture Source Components Signal Transport
Module 6 Credential module_6_badge.png

Troubleshooting (Primary Skill Module)

Develops a structured diagnostic mindset to resolve real classroom failure scenarios (no video, no audio, sync errors, or green screens). Follows a formal script: Identify → Isolate → Test → Resolve without guessing, while methodically tracking signal paths.

Diagnostic Mindset Signal Tracing Fault Isolation
Module 7 Credential module_7_badge.png

Installation Best Practices

Core principles of safe and efficient physical environment construction. Covers rack building, thermal cooling/airflow alignment, hardware display mounting, speaker placement, network config, and standard cable labeling techniques.

Rack Building Hardware Mounting Labeling Standards
Module 8 Credential course_complete_badge.png

Ongoing Professional Development

Long-term career roadmap building. Guides advanced student assistants toward expanding technical skill strategies, pursuing external industry benchmarks, and achieving formal professional certification opportunities.

Skill Strategy Industry Standards Certification Pathways
